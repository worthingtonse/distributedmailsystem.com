### How DMS Addresses Work
Each address follows this format:  
**Allegiance Level ~ Self-Describer . First Word . Second Word # Mailbox ID**

- **Allegiance Level**: Your "domain" that signals commitment (more below).
- **~ Self-Describer**: Optional! Express your identity, role, or personality (e.g., "CEO-VivaTech" or "Go-49ers!"). No periods, spaces, or quotes allowed.
- **. First Word . Second Word**: Automatically pulled from the name on your payment method (e.g., first and last name on your credit/debit card).
- **# Mailbox ID**: A unique random code assigned to your address.

### Break Free from Big Tech Email
Tired of centralized providers like Gmail or Outlook scanning your inbox, owning your data, and controlling your communication?

The **Distributed Mail System (DMS)** is fully decentralized — no single company controls it. Your address lives in the **Distributed Resource Directory (DRD)**, a public, distributed "host file" that anyone can access and verify.

### How DMS Prevents Spam and Abuse
To stop anyone from claiming thousands of addresses and spamming, every DMS address requires a **refundable stake**.

You deposit funds to "activate" your address — proving you're serious. If you stop using it, you get your money back. This economic incentive makes high-volume abuse expensive and impractical.

Higher stakes = higher trust. That's why we have **Allegiance Levels**:

| Level  | Stake Required | Status & Trust Signal                          | Best For                          |
|--------|----------------|-----------------------------------------------|-----------------------------------|
| **Bit**   | $10           | Entry level                                   | Casual users, testing             |
| **Byte**  | $20           | Basic commitment                              | Everyday personal email           |
| **Kilo**  | $50           | Moderate stake                                | Freelancers, small creators       |
| **Mega**  | $100          | Strong signal of legitimacy                   | Professionals, businesses         |
| **Giga**  | $1,000        | Highest trust — serious users only            | Executives, high-profile individuals |

A Giga-level user has far more "skin in the game" than a Bit-level one, making them much more trustworthy. Legitimate professionals are unlikely to risk a $1,000 stake (and their reputation) on spam.

While DMS has no built-in reputation system yet, third parties can easily build tools (e.g., open-rate boosters or filters) based on allegiance levels.
